PORT OF BEIRA

CORNELDER DE MOÇAMBIQUE

Beira Port, located strategically in the center of the country, links directly, by road or by rail, the main markets of the southern African hinterland, namely Zimbabwe, Botswana, Malawi, Zambia and Democratic Republic of Congo to the marketing and routes of international trade.

CONTAINER - TERMINAL

Multi-purpose container terminal

This is one of the most modern Terminals in southern Africa. The facilities include a 645 metre long quay with a depth of 12 metres. The terminal has 4 container gantry-cranes, two of which have the capacity to carry 65 tonnes.


The terminal can store more than 10,000 TEU's and has 148 electricity connection points for refrigerated containers. Currently, the terminal can handle 300,00 TEU's a year.

GENERAL CARGO - TERMINAL

Multi-purpose container terminal

The General Cargo Terminal covers 4 quays with a length of 670 metres and a depth of 9.5 metres. This is a multi-purpose Terminal and is duly equipped to handle a variety of bulk cargoes.


It contains five covered warehouses, with a total capacity of 15,000 m2, in addition to 12,000 m2 of open paved space for storing ferro-chrome, granite, steel and other bulk cargoes. There are rail connections to all the warehouses.

BEIRA GRAIN TERMINAL

All handling grain cargo solution

This terminal, although it is not within the limits of the concession area and so is not managed by CdM, allows the loading and unloading of bulk grain in more effective and efficient manner, with a productivity of between 4,000 an 5,000 Tonnes a day.


Initially, this terminal had a storage capacity (in silo) of 30,000 tonnes, but has been increased to 50,000 tonnes. Wheat and maize are the products most commonly handled annually by user of the terminal.
